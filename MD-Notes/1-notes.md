# Affective computing Week 1

> Lecture 1 - Fundamentals of AFC

## Defination
The field of affective computing encompasses both the creation of and interaction with machine systems that sense, recognize, respond to, 
and influence emotions (Picard,1997; Picard and Klein, 2002).
```
Creation and Interaction with machines that has the ability to sense, recognize and respond
```

```
Speech
Facial recognition
Lip reading
Regulation and control
Game play and social skills
```

## Affect sensing
- System can recognize the emotion by getting the data in terms of signals and patters
- Hardware + Software
- Face, Pose, Gesture, Hand tension, Vocal, Textual
- Physiological Signals
  - EDA Electrodermal activity
  - ECG Electroencephalography
  
### Application
- Detect (Detect the emotion)
- Expression (Sys express what human would preceive)
- Perception (Sys can feel the emotion)

## Affect sensing - Facial
- Low cost and easy to use
- Privacy issue (Age, Gender)
- Color thermal (Low resolution)
- Get the pose, gesture

## Affect sensing - Vocal
- Audio and BG noise
- Speech recognition

## Affect sensing - Modalities
1. Textual
   - Docs, emails, messages and social media
   - Conversation (human-machine, human-human)

2. Phsiological Signals
   - EDA (Electrodermal activity) - Change in conductivity of skin due to increase in sweat glands
   - EEG (Electroencephalography) - Records the electric activity of brain
